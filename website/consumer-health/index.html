Consumer Health Data Notice

Notice covering workout, readiness, recovery, coaching, account sync, collaboration, and Apple Health-derived information that may reveal health status.

Scope

SuggestMeSome processes workout, readiness, recovery, and coaching information that may reveal health status. This notice is written for a U.S. release that includes Washington residents and other state consumer-health privacy requirements.

Categories and sources

  • Workout history and exercise performance
  • Readiness check-ins, daily coach records, and training-recovery summaries
  • Coaching, fatigue, adaptation, and progress outputs that may reveal health-related inferences
  • Coach notes, assignments, private program shares, private progress shares, and visibility settings
  • Optional Apple Health data you authorize
  • Account identifiers, invitee email addresses, notification preferences, and support records tied to privacy requests involving consumer health data

Consumer health data may come from the data you manually enter, workouts you log, optional Apple Health data you authorize, and the collaboration or privacy-rights workflows you initiate.

Purposes and sharing

SuggestMeSome uses this information to provide workout logging, coaching, recovery, analytics, collaboration, private sharing, privacy-rights, support, and optional push-notification features that you request.

Apple Health-derived recovery data stays on device in this release and is not sent to the sync backend. If you invite or connect with a coach or training partner, the specific records covered by your visibility settings or share action may be disclosed to that recipient.

SuggestMeSome does not use Apple Health data for advertising.

Your rights

  • Confirm whether SuggestMeSome is processing your consumer health data
  • Request access to or export of consumer health data, including categories of recipients where required by law
  • Request deletion of consumer health data
  • Withdraw consent for future collection or sharing where consent is the legal basis
  • Appeal a denied privacy request where applicable law requires it

Use the app or privacy choices page, or contact privacy@suggestmesome.app.

Important limits

Deleting local app data does not delete records stored in Apple Health. Revoking collaboration access stops future sharing, but recipients may already have seen previously shared content. Deletion requests may require reasonable time to propagate to backups or archives where permitted by law.
